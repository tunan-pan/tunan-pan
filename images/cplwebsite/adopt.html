Adopt a cat

You can search through a listing of all our residents in our Kingston Shelter as at July 2010.

If you are thinking about adopting a cat or kittens from the Cats Protection League, please contact us and we will get back to you as soon as possible.

There are lots of cats to look at and your selection should be given enough time. You may be making a 10 to 20 year commitment when you take your new pet home. Time spent in choosing is very important.

Talk with the CPL staff about the temperament of the animal you are interested in. The CPL wants to make sure that cats will be going to good homes, and that they are well matched to their new owners.

If you wish to adopt, you will be asked to fill out a Questionnaire and Agreement form. These are necessary to ensure, as far as it is possible to do so, a happy future for any cat or kitten for which a home is found by the CPL.

Cats are arriving and departing all the time from our Kingston shelter (although still not being adopted out as often as we'd like) but some will never get the home they've always wanted.

Some of our cats have special needs and will probably live with the CPL for the rest of their lives. Find out about our special needs cats and how you can help them through our Sponsorship Programme.
